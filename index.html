About Me

Who I am

I am a scientist, a mathematician, and a programmer. Together, these traits make me a problem solver and a data scientist. I graduated with degrees in kinesiology and data science; two subjects that span over many different domains all of which I am equally passionate and curious about. What drove me to data science is the power it holds at solving problems and better decision making.

What I Do

I learn about techniques and algorithms used in machine learning and apply them to solve problems. For example, I use Python and SQL to gather millions of rows of data and create pipelines to transform and process them into machine learning models like gradient boosted trees or neural networks. More importantly I understand the abstractions of these models, why they are useful, how they can be applied, what parameters are needed, and the positives/negatives or tradeoffs as compared to other models used to solve similar problems.

The bottom line: I optimize, make predictions, and generate insights used for business or research.

Object Detection

Object detection is a classic Computer Vision problem and one of the more efficient ways to solve it is with Single Shot Detectors (SSD). The goal of this project was to build an SSD using a Fully Convolutional Network (FCN). As a kid from the 90s I found an interesting dataset of about 1000 images of Pikachu to train on. The result is a working SSD that can successfully detect multiple objects on a test image with training time taking less than 4 minutes on GPUs.

Risk Management

Data Science can play a huge role in Risk Management. This project shows how to use approximately 60 million rows of historical transactional data from multiple data sources in order to predict the default risk of a home credit loan applicant. Engineering of over 600 features with gradient boosted tree model XGBoost and Bayesian Hyperparameter Optimization were used which resulted in an Area Under the ROC Curve (AUC) score of 0.794. This netted an increase of 0.12 when using traditional Logistic Regression.
